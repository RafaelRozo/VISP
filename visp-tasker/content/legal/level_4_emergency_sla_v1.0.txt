VISP/TASKER LEVEL 4 — EMERGENCY SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT
Version 1.0 — Effective Date: January 1, 2026

THIS SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT ("SLA") SUPPLEMENTS THE INDEPENDENT
CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT, PLATFORM TERMS OF SERVICE, AND LEVEL 3 CERTIFIED
PROFESSIONAL TERMS. BY ACCEPTING A LEVEL 4 EMERGENCY DESIGNATION, YOU
AGREE TO THE FOLLOWING ADDITIONAL TERMS. THIS SLA IS SUBJECT TO ZERO
TOLERANCE ENFORCEMENT.

1. LEVEL 4 — EMERGENCY PROVIDER DESIGNATION
1.1. Level 4 Emergency Providers are elite, SLA-bound professionals
     who provide 24/7 on-call emergency home services. These services
     address urgent situations that pose an immediate risk to property,
     health, or safety.
1.2. Examples of Level 4 emergency tasks include, but are not limited to:
     - Burst pipe and flooding mitigation
     - Gas leak detection and emergency shut-off
     - Electrical hazard remediation (exposed wiring, panel failures)
     - Furnace failure during extreme weather
     - Air conditioning failure during extreme heat events
     - Sewer backup and emergency drainage
     - Lock-out emergency services
     - Storm damage emergency repairs (roof, windows, structural)
     - Fire damage emergency stabilization
     - Carbon monoxide alarm response

2. ALL LEVEL 3 REQUIREMENTS APPLY
2.1. Level 4 Emergency Providers must meet ALL Level 3 Certified
     Professional requirements, including but not limited to:
     (a) Valid, current provincial/state trade licenses;
     (b) $2,000,000 minimum commercial general liability insurance;
     (c) Criminal Record Check with Vulnerable Sector Screening;
     (d) All applicable certifications and continuing education.

3. ON-CALL REQUIREMENTS
3.1. You must commit to a minimum on-call schedule through the Platform.
     On-call shifts are scheduled in blocks as follows:
     (a) Weekday shifts: minimum twelve (12) hours per shift;
     (b) Weekend shifts: minimum eight (8) hours per shift;
     (c) You must be available for a minimum of forty (40) on-call
         hours per week, unless a reduced schedule is approved in
         advance by VISP.
3.2. During on-call hours, you must:
     (a) Have your mobile device powered on and within reach at all
         times;
     (b) Be within your designated service radius;
     (c) Have your vehicle loaded with standard emergency equipment
         and supplies for your trade;
     (d) Be prepared to depart for a job site within the response
         time specified in Section 4.

4. RESPONSE TIME SLA — ZERO TOLERANCE
4.1. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT: You must acknowledge (accept or decline) an
     emergency dispatch within FIVE (5) MINUTES of notification.
4.2. DEPARTURE: You must depart for the job site within FIFTEEN (15)
     MINUTES of accepting the dispatch.
4.3. ARRIVAL: You must arrive at the job site within the estimated
     arrival time provided by the Platform (based on real-time traffic
     and distance calculations), plus a maximum grace period of TEN
     (10) MINUTES.
4.4. ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY: Failure to meet these response time
     requirements is subject to zero tolerance enforcement:
     (a) First violation: Written warning and mandatory review;
     (b) Second violation within ninety (90) days: Temporary
         suspension of Level 4 status for fourteen (14) days;
     (c) Third violation within one hundred eighty (180) days:
         Permanent revocation of Level 4 status. You may continue
         as a Level 3 provider if eligible.
4.5. Force majeure exceptions may be considered for documented extreme
     weather events, road closures, or other circumstances genuinely
     beyond your control, at VISP's sole discretion.

5. COMPENSATION
5.1. Level 4 Emergency Providers receive a base compensation starting
     at $150.00 CAD per hour, with the actual rate determined by:
     (a) The specific emergency type and severity;
     (b) Time of day (overnight and holiday premiums apply);
     (c) Dynamic pricing factors based on demand and availability;
     (d) Geographic region and travel requirements.
5.2. On-call standby compensation may be provided at a reduced hourly
     rate during scheduled on-call hours when no dispatch is received,
     as determined by VISP.
5.3. Overtime rates may apply for emergency jobs exceeding four (4)
     hours of continuous on-site work.

6. COMMISSION STRUCTURE
6.1. VISP charges a commission of fifteen percent (15%) to twenty-five
     percent (25%) on each completed Level 4 emergency job.
6.2. The higher commission range reflects the premium pricing, dynamic
     pricing capability, and the Platform's investment in maintaining
     the emergency dispatch infrastructure.
6.3. The specific commission rate for each job is displayed at dispatch.

7. EMERGENCY EQUIPMENT REQUIREMENTS
7.1. You must maintain a fully stocked emergency response kit
     appropriate to your trade at all times during on-call hours.
7.2. Minimum equipment requirements by trade are specified in the
     Tasker Emergency Provider Handbook and may be updated periodically.
7.3. VISP reserves the right to conduct random equipment audits.

8. COMMUNICATION REQUIREMENTS
8.1. You must provide real-time status updates through the Platform:
     (a) When you depart for the job site;
     (b) When you arrive at the job site;
     (c) Initial assessment of the situation;
     (d) When the emergency has been stabilized;
     (e) When the job is complete.
8.2. For jobs involving safety hazards (gas leaks, electrical hazards),
     you must confirm that the immediate hazard has been mitigated
     within the first thirty (30) minutes of arrival or escalate to
     emergency services (911).

9. PERFORMANCE METRICS
9.1. You must maintain:
     (a) Average response time at or below the SLA targets;
     (b) Customer rating of 4.5 out of 5.0 or higher;
     (c) Job acceptance rate of at least ninety-five percent (95%)
         during on-call hours;
     (d) Zero safety incidents attributable to negligence.

10. SAFETY AND ESCALATION
10.1. If an emergency situation poses an imminent threat to life, you
      must instruct the customer to call 911 immediately and may
      assist only to the extent safe and legally permitted.
10.2. You must not enter a property if you reasonably believe there
      is an immediate danger to your personal safety.
10.3. All safety incidents must be reported through the Platform
      within one (1) hour of occurrence.

11. IMMEDIATE TERMINATION
11.1. VISP may immediately and permanently revoke your Level 4 status
      without warning for:
      (a) Any safety incident caused by negligence;
      (b) Falsifying response times or status updates;
      (c) Abandoning an emergency job site before the situation is
          stabilized;
      (d) Operating without valid insurance or license;
      (e) Being under the influence of alcohol or drugs during
          on-call hours or while performing services.

BY ACCEPTING LEVEL 4 EMERGENCY PROVIDER STATUS, YOU CONFIRM THAT YOU
HAVE READ, UNDERSTOOD, AND AGREE TO THIS SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT,
INCLUDING THE ZERO TOLERANCE ENFORCEMENT PROVISIONS. YOU UNDERSTAND
THAT EMERGENCY SERVICES CARRY HEIGHTENED RESPONSIBILITIES AND THAT
FAILURE TO MEET SLA REQUIREMENTS WILL RESULT IN CONSEQUENCES AS
DESCRIBED HEREIN.
